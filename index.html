Background and Theme

Logic has been an old area of study since ancient time. Nowadays it exhibits its power in two areas: (1) in computer science, formal methods based on logic greatly improves reliability of hardware and software, (2) in mathematics and philosophy, logic reveals deep and inherent properties about essentially all of human's and computer's reasoning activities by influential theorems like Gödel's incompleteness theorems, undecidability of the halting problem, etc. My research concerns those two aspects. Specifically, I mainly study the following areas.

  • Fully automated formal methods, with a focus on program analysis and model checking
  • Theory of computation, with a focus on computability theory and relations between computations and proofs

I'm also broadly interested in many related areas, such as computer-aided proofs, computational commplexity theory, etc.
